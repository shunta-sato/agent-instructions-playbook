# Textbook note-type contracts

Use these contracts for paraphrased textbook-style learning notes. Do not copy long passages from the source. Prefer paraphrased explanation, compact references, and original learning structure. Use short quotations only when necessary and only within applicable copyright limits.

## Shared requirements for non-index notes

Every non-index note must include:

- what it is
- why it matters in the source argument
- when a practitioner would use or observe it
- inputs, forces, or conditions
- output, decision, or diagnosis enabled
- failure or misuse mode
- next reader action
- semantic links to related notes

## Root/index note

Purpose: orient the pack and expose the learning taxonomy.

Required content:

- source classification and scope
- map of chapters, concepts, methods, patterns, antipatterns, and reviews
- reading path for first pass, application pass, and review pass
- links to major notes and review prompts
- publish/sync readiness status when relevant

## Chapter note

Purpose: explain the chapter-level argument and how its units fit together.

Required content:

- chapter problem or theme
- main concepts, methods, patterns, or antipatterns introduced
- why the chapter matters in the source argument
- conditions where the chapter's ideas apply
- outputs, decisions, or diagnoses enabled by the chapter
- common misunderstanding or misuse
- next reader action and review prompts
- semantic links to child and sibling notes

## Concept note

Purpose: make a stable idea usable, not merely defined.

Required content:

- concise paraphrased explanation
- role in the source taxonomy or argument
- use or observation situation
- inputs, forces, or conditions that make it relevant
- decision, diagnosis, or language it enables
- example shape when abstraction would be unclear
- failure or misuse mode
- next reader action
- links to methods, patterns, antipatterns, and reviews

## Method/process note

Purpose: describe a repeatable way to act or analyze.

Required content:

- what the method/process does
- why it matters in the source argument
- when to use it and when not to use it
- inputs, preconditions, forces, or constraints
- steps plus the decision each step enables
- output, decision, or diagnosis produced
- pitfalls, misuse, or missing-input failure mode
- next reader action or practice prompt
- links to concepts, examples, patterns, and reviews

## Pattern note

Purpose: capture a reusable solution shape.

Required content:

- pattern intent
- problem and context
- forces or tradeoffs
- solution structure, paraphrased
- resulting decision, behavior, or diagnosis
- applicability conditions and limits
- consequences and failure modes
- next reader action
- links to related concepts, methods, antipatterns, and mini-patterns

## Antipattern note

Purpose: capture a recurring harmful solution shape and recovery path.

Required content:

- antipattern symptom
- context where it appears attractive
- forces that sustain it
- harm or consequence
- diagnosis enabled
- corrective action or replacement pattern
- misuse mode when diagnosing too broadly
- next reader action
- links to related concepts, patterns, and review prompts

## Mini-pattern / mini-antipattern note

Purpose: capture a small reusable move or smell that is too narrow for a full pattern note.

Required content:

- compact intent or symptom
- triggering condition
- action or warning
- decision or diagnosis enabled
- common misuse
- next reader action
- links to parent pattern, antipattern, method, or review note

## Review note

Purpose: help the reader test recall, transfer, and application.

Required content:

- scope covered
- retrieval prompts tied to concepts and methods
- application questions with concrete conditions
- contrast questions for patterns and antipatterns
- common failure modes to check
- links back to notes needed for remediation
