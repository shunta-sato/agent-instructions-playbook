# Technical essay note contracts

Use these contracts for paraphrased technical essay learning notes. Do not copy long passages from the source. Prefer paraphrased explanation, compact references, and original learning structure. Use short quotations only when necessary and only within applicable copyright limits.

## Essay note

Purpose: preserve a complete argument unit.

Required content:

- claim
- situation/context
- reasoning path
- supporting example or anecdote, paraphrased
- practitioner action
- assumptions that may be time-bound
- limits/counterpressure
- present-day applicability
- links to practice, claim, and historical-context notes

## Principle/claim note

Purpose: make an author's claim testable and reusable.

Required content:

- claim in paraphrased form
- reasoning that supports it
- contexts where it applies
- assumptions and time-bound dependencies
- counterpressure or competing principle
- practical implication
- links to essays, practices, and checklists

## Practice/method note

Purpose: turn advice into an action pattern without losing context.

Required content:

- practice or method
- originating claim or essay
- situation where it helps
- steps or operating questions
- expected output or decision
- limits and failure modes
- present-day applicability note
- links to claims, essays, and checklists

## Strategy/market note

Purpose: capture organizational, product, or market advice with context.

Required content:

- strategic claim
- market or organizational context
- reasoning path
- time-bound assumptions
- signals that the advice still applies or no longer applies
- risks and counterpressure
- links to essays, historical contexts, and practices

## Historical-context note

Purpose: separate historical explanation from timeless advice.

Required content:

- historical or technology context
- role in the essay's reasoning
- what has likely changed
- what may remain durable
- applicability limit
- links to essays, claims, and practices

## Checklist note

Purpose: support application and review.

Required content:

- scope and source essays
- checks phrased as actions or diagnostic questions
- applicability limits
- evidence needed before using the checklist
- failure modes and counterpressure
- links to supporting essays, claims, practices, and historical contexts

## Review/index note

Purpose: orient the pack and support transfer.

Required content:

- source classification
- essay, claim, practice, context, and checklist map
- reading paths by theme and application need
- present-day applicability summary
- links to semantic review and mechanical gate artifacts when relevant
